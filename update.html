EMPOWERING LIFELONG LEARNERS

ALS 2.0

In 2016, the ALS underwent significant reforms, culminating in ALS 2.0. This involved extensive consultations, curriculum revisions, and the development of a five-year strategic roadmap. The enhanced ALS K to 12 Basic Education Curriculum was finalized, aligning with the formal K to 12 system while remaining adaptable to the diverse needs of ALS learners.

ALS Program Structure

The ALS program offers both elementary and secondary levels. Instruction can be school-based or community-based, utilizing various learning centers and resources. The curriculum is modular and flexible, focusing on six learning strands: communication skills (English and Filipino), scientific literacy and critical thinking, mathematical and problem-solving skills, life and career skills, understanding the self and society, and digital citizenship. Learners undergo a functional literacy test to determine their starting point and create an individual learning agreement (ILA) to track progress.

Accreditation and Equivalency (A&E) Test

The Alternative Learning System (ALS) in the Philippines uses an Accreditation and Equivalency (A&E) test to assess the learning competencies of out-of-school youth and adults (OSYAs). This test allows individuals who haven't completed formal schooling to gain recognition for their knowledge and skills.
