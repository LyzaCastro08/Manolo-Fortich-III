Alternative Learning System - ALS

EMPOWERING LIFELONG LEARNERS

What is Alternative Learning System or ALS?

It is a parallel learning system in the Philippines that provides a practical option to the existing formal instruction. When one does not have or cannot access formal education in schools, ALS is an alternate or substitute. ALS includes both the non-formal and informal sources of knowledge and skills. It offers the following:

The Basic Literacy Program (BLP)

It is a program component of ALS aimed at eradicating illiteracy among OSYA, and in extreme cases, school-aged children, by developing the basic literacy skills of reading, writing, and numeracy.

Program for dropouts of formal Elementary and Secondary Levels: Continuing Education: Accreditation and Equivalency (A&E) Program

The Accreditation and Equivalency (A&E) Program is a program aimed at providing an alternative pathway of learning for out-of-school children, youth and adults who are basically literate but who have not completed the 10 years of basic education mandated by the Philippine Constitution. Through this program, school dropouts are able to complete elementary and high school education outside the formal school system.

Community-Based Learning:

ALS often emphasizes community-based learning, promoting self-help and community development.
